About LCCF

Lila's Child Care Foundation started in 2004 and has been operating as a free school for many years. Not only does LCCF give free tuition and school supplies but LCCF also provides food (breakfast and lunch), clothes, shoes, school bags and other items for the underprivileged children who would not have been able to receive an education if not for LCCF. To make parents a little responsible and involved in their child's well-being, LCCF decided that parents support their children with a contribution of GH₵40 (about $5) termly - three to four months - because they cannot afford so much. Unfortunately, in 2021, LCCf had to move from its location due to land reclaim by the owner. Currently, LCCF has moved to an acre of land in the Volta Region. With the help of a few donors, the foundation has started its New Beginning School project - a three unit classroom block with toilet facilities and a kitchen for start.

The Founder

Lila Macqueen Djaba founded the Lila's Child Care Foundation in 2004 after many years of volunteerism. She completed high school at Akwamuman Secondary School. She also graduated with degrees in early childhood education, radio broadcasting and IT from National Nursery Teachers Training College and Rabodef Radio Academy. She started volunteering after high school at a Liberian refugee camp for six months. After, she volunteered at Street Academy and the Osu Children's Home each for a year. She also provided easy access to potable water at the Tamale Children's home through the construction of a borehole for the orphanage. For her impact on the lives of over one hundred and seventy (170) children through the foundation, she was awarded the "Heroes of Change" award by MTN. Lila Macqueen Djaba currently oversees the New Beginning School project for the construction of a 3-unit classroom block in the Volta Region.

New Beginnings School Project Description
New Beginnings School Project

The New beginning school project was embarked on by Lila's Child Care Foundation after relocating to the Volta Region from Ashaiman. The project involves the construction of a three-unit classroom block with toilet facilities and a kitchen for starters. The three-unit classroom block will be used to provide education to underprivileged children in the Volta Region who cannot afford an education due to financial constraints.
After further upgrades in the future, the project will also aim to offer vocational training to children, giving them a skill they can use to make a living for themselves.

Lila's Child Care Foundation Library Description
Lila's Child Care Foundation Library

The Lila's Child Care Foundation Library project was embarked on and completed when the foundation was still located at Ashaiman. The library was built to provide the children access to books for teaching them to read, cultivating good reading habits and building their knowledge. The library was built in a container, making it portable. The portability of the library saved the foundation from constructing a new library upon relocating to the Volta Region.
